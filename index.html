From idea to impact

Bring an AI idea to life with AI Leap

Teams start with a real client, delivery or internal problem. AI Leap helps them shape the idea, check readiness, and route it to the right path: self-serve guidance, SME support, Leap Studio or a Leap Hackathon.

Common journey before the route is chosen

The team does not need to know upfront whether Studio or Hackathon is right. The route depends on readiness, support need, timing, theme fit, urgency and ownership.

User question

Where could AI help us solve a real problem?

The journey starts with a specific client, delivery or internal pain point, not with a technology experiment. The team should identify where work is slow, repetitive, inconsistent, hard to scale or difficult to quality check.

Multiple entry points are intentional

Teams may come through the Hub when they have an idea, through a Hackathon email when a cohort is announced, or through a referral from a sponsor, SME, project lead or colleague. The readiness check creates a common standard after these different starting points.

Route recommendation

The route is based on objective readiness and practical context. The same front door can send a team to self-serve guidance, SME shaping, Studio, Hackathon or the backlog.

Recommended when

Leap Studio

The use case is ready to build, has a clear owner and sponsor, has a safe data path, and needs focused support to produce a working demo and business case within a short sprint window.

Ready nowClear ownerStructured build
Trigger

Team has a clear use case and wants to move now.

Support intensity

Medium to high. Structured sprint plus targeted SME support.

Output

Working demo, business case and next-step decision.

Risk if misrouted

Studio becomes generic office hours if readiness is too weak.

Routing factor What to check Implication
Readiness Problem clarity, value case, owner, sponsor, data path, tooling path High readiness points toward Studio or Hackathon. Low readiness points toward self-serve or SME shaping
Support need How much structure, facilitation or specialist input the team needs Light need can be self-serve. High need may require SME support or Studio
Timing Whether the idea must move now or can wait for a cohort window Urgent ready use cases fit Studio. Event-timed use cases may fit Hackathon
Theme fit Whether the idea matches an active Hackathon theme, platform, client group or capability priority Strong theme fit and cohort value point toward Hackathon
Post-demo ownership Who will own the next step after the demo No owner means no sprint until ownership is resolved

What happens after routing

Use the tabs to explore each route once the Hub, announcement or referral has brought the team into the common readiness process.

Studio role

Studio is the standard build route for ready use cases. It starts when the idea is ready, not when the next event window opens.

SBest fit: clear use case, committed owner, sponsor, safe data path, approved tools and a need to build now.
Studio stage

Studio intake

The team confirms what it will build, who owns it, who sponsors it, what data and tools are needed, and what a successful demo should prove.

Sprint briefOwnerSponsor
Team action

Submit the intake and commit team availability.

Leap action

Validate fit and confirm sprint setup.

Output

Confirmed Studio sprint brief.

Watchout

Do not start Studio without a clear owner and a realistic demo target.

Hackathon role

Hackathon is the occasional cohort route. It creates momentum, peer learning, leadership visibility and side-by-side comparison of use cases.

HBest fit: idea fits a theme or event window, team can commit to the timeline, and the output benefits from visibility and cohort energy.
Hackathon stage

Fit check

The team checks whether the idea fits the Hackathon theme, eligibility rules, timeline, guardrails and expected deliverables.

Theme fitTimelineGuardrails
Team action

Review event page and confirm whether the idea fits.

Leap action

Publish clear eligibility, dates, rules and outputs.

Output

Watchout

Do not force ideas into a theme just because an event is visible.

SME and self-serve role

Not every idea should go straight into a sprint. Early or uncertain ideas should be shaped first, either through self-serve tools or targeted SME input.

?Best fit: the idea has potential but needs clearer value, sharper scope, safer data path, tooling advice or sponsor alignment.
Path When it fits What the team does What happens next
Self-serve guidance Idea is early, support need is low, or the team is still exploring the problem Use AI Leap Hub templates, examples, readiness prompts and guardrails Return to readiness check once the idea is clearer
SME support Idea is credible but needs challenge on business value, data, tools, risk, feasibility or scale Request targeted SME support with a short problem brief Route to Studio, Hackathon, continued shaping or backlog
Backlog or revisit Idea lacks a clear owner, value case, data path or realistic build target Clarify missing conditions before asking for sprint support Reassess when ownership, value or readiness improves
